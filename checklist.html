Checklist for Incorporating the Business into a Private Limited Company

  • Step 1: Choose a Company Name
    • 1.1 Review the Companies House naming guideline.

      Your company name must follow Companies House rules.

      The name must be unique and distinguishable.

      It must not be offensive, misleading, or contain certain restricted words without permission.

      • Example: to use ‘Accredited’ in your company’s name, you need permission from the Department for Business and Trade (DBT).

      Guidance on naming your company and which words you need permission to use

    • 1.2 Ensure the company’s name is not identical, the “same as” or “too like” an existing trade name.

      ‘Same as’ names

      • A ‘same as’ name differs only by punctuation, special characters, or common words.
      • Example: ‘Hands UK Ltd’ and ‘Hand’s Ltd’ are considered the same as ‘Hands Ltd’.
      • You can use a ‘same as’ name only if your company is part of the same group as the existing company and you have written confirmation of no objection.

      ‘Too like’ names

      • A ‘too like’ name may require a change if someone complains.
      • Example: ‘Easy Electrics For You Ltd' is considered too like ‘EZ’ Electrix 4U Ltd’.

      You can check your company’s name availability using Companies House Name Checker

    • 1.3 Trading names (using a different name to your registered company name)

      You can trade under a different name than your registered company name. This is known as a ‘business name’.

      • Business Name Must Not include:
        • ‘Limited’, ‘Ltd’, ‘LLP’, ‘plc’ (or similar legal endings).
        • ‘Sensitive’ words without permission.
      • Trade mark Conflicts:
        • If your business name is too similar to another company’s trademark, you may be required to change it if someone complains.
        • Check Trademarks here.
    • 1.4 Rules for displaying your company name

      Signs

      • You must display a sign showing your company name at your registered company address and at all locations where your business operates.
        • However, if you are running your business from home, displaying a sign there is not required.
        • Examples: if you are running 3 shops and an office (not at home), you must display a sign at each location.
        • The sign must be easy to read and visible at all times, not just when you are open.

      Stationery and promotional Material

      • Your company’s name must be included on all company documents, publicity materials, and letters, which must show:
        • Your company’s registered number.
        • Its registered office address.
        • Where the company is registered (e.g., England and Wales, Scotland, or Northern Ireland).
      • Check the rules you must follow about displaying your company name here.
  • Step 2: Choose directors and a company secretary

    Your company name must follow Companies House rules.

    The name must be unique and distinguishable.

    It must not be offensive, misleading, or contain certain restricted words without permission.

    • Example: to use ‘Accredited’ in your company’s name, you need permission from the Department for Business and Trade (DBT).

    Guidance on naming your company and which words you need permission to use

    You must appoint a director but you do not have to appoint a company secretary.

    • 2.1 Appoint directors

      Your company must have at least one director (which can be yourself).

      A director must be 16 or over and not disqualified from being a director.

      You should provide below details of the director (which are publicly available

      from Companies House):

      Full name

      Date of birth

      Address

      Nationality

      Occupation

      Directors must provide 2 addresses:

      A service address (can be the same as the registered office address of the

      company, or it can be somewhere different).

      Usual residential address (the director’s home address, which will not be

      available on the public register for everyone to see).

      You could check your personal information on the Companies House register

      here.

    • 2.2 Directors’ responsibilities

       As a director of a limited company, you must:

      follow the company’s rules, shown in its articles of association (see Step 4).

      keep company records and report changes

      • 2.2.1 Companies records (see Step 5)
      • 2.2.2 Report changes
        • You must report certain changes to Companies House, e.g., changing your company’s registered office address, changing your company’s registered email address etc.
        • For further details, please click here.
        • You can report any changes to the Companies House through here.

        file your accounts and your Company Tax Return

      • 2.2.3 To file your accounts and your Company Tax Return, you will need your:
        • HMRC online account details
        • Company Registration number
        • Companies House online account details.
        • For further details, please click here.

        tell other shareholders if you might personally benefit from a transaction the company makes

        pay Corporation Tax

        • You must pay Corporation Tax on profits from doing business as a limited company.
        • Taxable profits for Corporation Tax include the money your company or association makes from doing business, investments, and selling assets for more than they cost.
        • For further details, please click here.
    • 2.3 Company secretary

      You do not need a company secretary for a private limited company.

      • Some companies use them to take on some of the directors’ responsibilities.
  • Step 3: Decide who the shareholders or guarantors are

    Most limited companies are ‘limited by shares’, meaning they are owned by shareholders who hold shares and have voting rights.

    • 3.1 Share particulars

      Every limited company must have at least one class of ordinary shares that cannot be redeemed.

      • This means that the company cannot buy back those shares from shareholder at any point in the future.

      At least one of these irredeemable shares must be in effect when incorporating the company.

    • 3.2 Understanding share ownership

      Most companies issue ‘ordinary’ shares, giving shareholders:

      • One vote per share on company decisions.
      • Dividend payments when profits are distributed.

      A company limited by shares must have at least one shareholder (who can also be the director).

      • No maximum number of shareholders.
      • This means that you can be the sole member, the sole shareholder, and the sole director of the company.
      • Share price can be any value, e.g., £1 per share.
    • 3.3 Issuing your initial shares

      When registering a company, you must provide a statement of capital, which

      includes:

      The number of shares issued and their total value (company’s ‘share capital’).

      The names and addresses of all shareholders – known as ‘subscribers’ or ‘members’

      • Example: if a company issues 500 shares at £1 each, the share capital is £500.
    • 3.4 Prescribed particulars (Shareholder’s rights)

      You must include details on each share type’s rights, and must include:

      The share of dividends shareholders get.

      Share value, are their shares exchangeable (‘redeemable’) for money?

      Shareholder’s eligibility to vote on certain company matters.

      Voting results.

    • 3.5 Identify people with significant control (PSC) over your company.

      A PSC is someone who owns or has major influence over your company.

      If you run a private limited company, you are legally required to:

      Identify at least one PSC (if applicable).

      Keep a PSC register (a record of who controls your company).

      Submit PSC details when setting up your company

      • 3.5.1 Identifying a PSC

        A PSC meets at least one of the following conditions:

        Holds more than 25% of shares in the company.

        Holds more than 25% of voting rights.

        Has the right to appoint or remove a majority of directors.

        Exercises significant influence or control over the company.

      • 3.5.2 Recording PSC information

         When setting up your company, you must provide:

        Full name

        Date of birth

        Nationality and country of residence

        Correspondence address (publicly available)

        Home address (non-disclosable)

        The date they become PSC of the company

        The date you entered them into your PSC register

        All natures of control which apply

        Level of their shares and voting rights

      • 3.5.3 Keep your PSC register updated here 

        Changes must be recorded within 14 days.

        Updates must be sent to Companies House within another 14 days.

      • 3.5.4 Compliance and penalties
        • Failure to provide PSC information is a criminal offence.
        • Providing false PSC details can result in a fine or up to 2 years in prison.
        • Refusing to disclose PSC details allows the company to restrict their shares or voting rights.

        For further details of Step 3.1-3.4, click here. For Step 3.5, click here.

  • Step 4: Memorandum and Articles of Association

    You must prepare two key legal documents when registering your company:

    Memorandum of Association

    • A legal statement signed by all initial shareholders agreeing to form the company.

    Articles of Association

    • The rules for running the company, agreed upon by shareholders, guarantors, directors, and the company secretary (if applicable).
    • 4.1 Memorandum of Association

      If you register online, the memorandum is created automatically during registration.

      If you register by post, you can use the memorandum of association template.

      • You cannot update the memorandum once the company has been registered.
    • 4.2 Articles of Association

       You can choose one of two options:

      Use standard ‘model articles’ (provided by Companies House)

      OR

      Write your own and upload or send them when you register your company.

  • Step 5: Check what records you will need to keep
    • 5.1 Company records

      You must keep details of, e.g. information of directors or shareholders, results of any shareholder votes and resolutions.

      • A comprehensive list of details record that you need to keep can be found here.
    • 5.2 Register of ‘People with Significant Control’ (Step 3.5)
    • 5.3 Accounting records

      You must keep accounting records that include, e.g., details of assets owned by the company, debts the company owes or is owed etc.

      • For further details, please click here.
    • 5.4 Duration of kept records

      Your company’s records must be kept for at least 6 years from the end of the financial year they relate to.

      You may need to keep them longer if:

      • They cover transactions spanning multiple accounting periods.
      • The company owns long-term assets, e.g., equipment or machinery.
      • You filed your Company Tax Return late.
      • HMRC is conducting a compliance check.
    • 5.5 Lost, stolen, or destroyed records

      If your records are lost, stolen, or destroyed, you must:

      Try to recreate them as best as possible.

      Inform HMRC’s Corporation Tax Office immediately.

      Include an explanation in your Company Tax Return.

  • Step 6: Register your company with Companies House
    • 6.1 Your company’s official address

      Your company must have a UK registered office address.

      The address must be a physical location (not a P.O Box).

      • Companies House and HMRC will send statutory/official mail to this address.
      • The address will be publicly visible on Companies House through the Companies House Search Service.

      Keeping your address private

      • If you do not want your home address to be public, you should not use it as your company’s registered office.
    • 6.2 Your registered email address

      You must provide a company email address when registering (which will not be made public).

      You must monitor this email regularly.

      • Companies House may use it to contact you about official company matters.
    • 6.3 Choose a Standard Industrial Classification (SIC) code

      You are required to determine your business activity and its corresponding SIC

      code.

      You can find the official SIC code list here.

    • 6.4 Registering your company

      You must provide at least 3 pieces of personal information for yourself and your shareholders, such as:

      Town of birth

      Mother’s maiden name

      Father’s first name

      Telephone number

      National Insurance Number

      Passport Number

      It cost £50 and can be paid by debit or credit card.

      Your company is usually registered within 24 hours.

    • 6.5 Additional services when registering online

       Register for PAYE (if employing staff, including yourself).

      Continuing an existing registration application.

      If you do not want to use ‘Limited’ in your company name, you must register by post.

    • 6.6 Ways to register

      By post (using Form IN01, 8-10 days, £71.00)

      Using an agent

      Using third-party software

  • Step 7: Manage your Corporation Tax

    You should add Corporation Tax services to your account when you start to do business.

    • 7.1 Add Corporation Tax to your business tax account

      You will need:

      Your company’s registration number.

      The date you started your business (your company’s first accounting period will start from this date).

      The date you set up your first account.

      Your company’s 10-digit Unique Taxpayer Reference (UTR) - you can find your UTR on any letter from HMRC.

      • If you did not get a UTR 15 working days after registering your company, you can request your company’s UTR online.
    • 7.2 How to add Corporation Tax services

      Sign in to your business tax account using your Government Gateway user ID and password. If you do not have a user ID, you can create one when you sign in.

      Select ‘Services you can add’ in the left hand menu.

      Find the Corporation Tax service and select ‘Enrol for service’.
